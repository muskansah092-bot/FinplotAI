CORE FINANCIAL PLANNING PRIORITIES:
1. Emergency Fund: A user must maintain 3-6 months of essential living expenses before prioritizing discretionary milestone purchases.
2. Debt Management: High-interest debt (annual interest rate greater than 12%) must be paid down aggressively before funding secondary lifestyle goals.
3. Priority Queue: Emergency Fund > High-Interest Debt > Lifestyle Purchases.

BUDGETING FRAMEWORKS & SAVINGS STRATEGIES:
- 50/30/20 Rule: Allocate 50% of net income to Needs, 30% to Wants, and 20% to Savings/Financial Goals.
- Variable Outlay Reduction: If a goal monthly target exceeds current disposable income, audit variable categories (e.g., Shopping, Food Delivery, Subscriptions) and recommend a maximum 40% reduction in those specific areas to free up capital.
- Timeline Extension Strategy: If the required monthly target exceeds disposable income even after variable reductions, scale the timeline out dynamically (e.g., extending an 8-month goal to 18 months) to drop the required target within realistic boundaries. Do not recommend high-interest financing/loans if the financial health score is below 60.
