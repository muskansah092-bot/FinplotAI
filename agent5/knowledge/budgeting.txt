BUDGETING GUIDELINES

1. A commonly recommended split is the 50/30/20 rule:
   - 50% of income for needs (rent, groceries, utilities, EMIs)
   - 30% for wants (entertainment, dining out, shopping)
   - 20% for savings and investments

2. If monthly expenses exceed 70% of income, it usually signals a need to cut discretionary spending.

3. Track spending by category monthly to identify where overspending is happening.

4. Set a fixed "fun money" limit each month to avoid impulsive spending without feeling overly restricted.

5. Renegotiate or review recurring subscriptions and memberships every 6 months — many go unused.

6. For people with dependents, budgeting should also account for education and healthcare costs as separate line items, not lumped into general expenses.
